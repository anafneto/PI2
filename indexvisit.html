Viseu Cathedral began to be built in the 12th century and, over the years, various pieces have been added, resulting in a combination of styles including Romanesque, Manueline, Renaissance and Gothic.

Igreja da Misericórdia’s chambers are filled with priceless oil paintings and holy objects. Some are portraits of the numerous donors to the church, while others feature religious themes or figures.

40° 39′ 37″ N, 7° 54′ 43″ W

At the heart of Viseu’s historical centre, the bronze statue of King Duarte presides over a square lined with cafés and restuarants and backed by the cathedral walls. It’s a great place for people watching and admiring the beautiful old buildings that surround the square.

40° 39' 34.80" N, 7° 54' 41.61" W

Praça da República is leafy and flower-filled, with a fountain and merry-go-round. It’s also bordered on one side by an azulejo mural. During festivals and events, there are usually activities in this square but even without extra thrills, the café in the centre is a popular spot with locals.

Rua Direita is well-known for its charmingly retro stores, which are like stepping into a delightful time capsule. These shops are a haven for vintage lovers and offer a truly unique shopping experience that captures the charm of yesteryear.
